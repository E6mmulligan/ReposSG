About Me

My work ethic stems from growing up on a small horse farm in PA, where feeding and caring for animals and the farm itself was a responsibility 24/7/365. My first career was being a Tennis Professional at some of the top-ranked clubs in the United States, using my work ethic to out-perform peers and staff in senior job titles. I thrive on creating goals that are difficult to achieve, and then achieving them.

My character is shaped from my faith, my family, and my passion to overcome challenges in life. My strengths include honesty, integrity, a drive to improve, creation of win-win scenarios for all parties involved, and a positive attitude, among others. I pride myself on both the profitable tennis acadamy I started and operated for seven years, as well as the unprofitable regional marketing company I started. I learned from both experiences, and I am a better person today because of both of these companies.

Experiences

For 20 years, I utilized my dual-major Bachelor of Science degree from Ferris State University, in both Marketing and Professional Tennis Management, as a Tennis Pro. I have coached tennis students of all ages, and in abilities ranging from beginner to a professional on the WTA (Women's Pro Tour). I have "given back" to the community as a volunteer coach for the MaliVai Washington Foundation in Jacksonville, FL. Helping other people enjoy life, building confidence in themselves, and realizing their fullest potential on- and off-court continues to be a passion of mine as I have migrated from Tennis Coach to Entrepreneur to Consultant to Mentor.

After making significant contributions to four of the top-150 ranked private membership clubs in the United States, including being recognized as teaching the most lessons at the #2-ranked club in USA, my next goal is to scale larger. Instead of helping 1-100 people simultaneously, like I can do while teaching tennis or running tennis events, I want to scale larger to help thousands or millions of people simultaneously, by creating Java programs that enrich lives of other people, while being scalable for growth beyond what I can imagine today, and while creating a profit for my organization. I believe that the ultimate commodities are time and health. I dream of creating efficiencies for people, via Java programming, so that people can spend more of their time healthly, shared with their loved ones.

Hobbies

Tennis remains a passion for me. I enjoy teaching tennis, especially to children ages 6-12, where I feel I can help shape their confidence in all aspects of their lives. Additionally, I enjoy helping adults to remember that tennis improvement will happen as a byproduct of keeping the FUN included in their tennis play!

Other hobbies include travels, learning, philosophy, art, photography, writing, nutrition, helping others, math, nature, movies, and reflecting about famous quotes that inspire me. One of these quotes is a portion of a poem by Samuel Beckett, "Ever tried. Ever failed. No matter. Try again. Fail again. Fail better."
